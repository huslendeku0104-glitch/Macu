The beginning

In 1995, Tumurkhuyag Urtnasan established Ligiin Ukhaa LLC and began to make his now famous Khustai Gouda. A few other small cheesemakers followed. Facing daunting issues of marketing, sales, distribution and capital, none of these small cheesemakers were able to grow their businesses. Most failed. Tumurkhuyag himself was on the cusp of bankruptcy in May 2014 when Michael Morrow negotiated and settle his bank debt. In 2015 Morrow and his wife, Wang Hongyan, set up Mongolian Artisan Cheesemakers Union (MACU) and tried to persuade Tumurkhuyag and the few other remaining cheesemakers to invest. None were willing. MACU was established anyway and a plan formulated for developing a network of 100 small cheese plants.
